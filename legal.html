The ClozeSure Guarantee Policy Summary

Some important facts about your Home Sellers Property Completion Financial Guarantee - ClozeSure - are summarised below. This summary does not describe all the terms and conditions of the policy. To ensure full understanding of the cover provided we recommend that this document is read alongside the Guarantee wording.

Financial Guarantor

This Financial Guarantee policy has been arranged by Contract Completion Company Ltd and is underwritten by XXX. Registered Office: XXX XXX XXX. Registered in England and Wales No. XXXXXX.

Eligibility

You are eligible for this Financial Guarantee provided that, at the start date:
You are aged 18 or over
You are selling a property in England, Wales or Northern Ireland
You are the owner of the property
Your buyer is a private individual/s (i.e. not buying your property on behalf of a corporate entity or trust),
You are unconnected to the buyer of your property
Your buyer’s deposit, payable on exchange of contracts, is at least 10% of the agreed sale price of your property
Your property is being sold with vacant possession on completion date,
You are using a solicitor or licensed conveyancer to conduct the conveyancing.

We will not provide any cover if you do not meet these eligibility requirements at the start date of your policy.

Claim Events

This Financial Guarantee, subject to the terms and conditions, provides cover if your property sale does not complete during the period of cover due to your Buyer’s failure to complete, through no fault of your own and for reasons beyond your control, their contractually-agreed purchase of your property, in the time-frame set out by your Agreement of Sale contract and the subsequent Completion Notice served on your defaulting Buyer by your conveyancer.

The Financial Guarantor will pay you:
the 90% balance of the sale price of your property due from your ‘failed’ buyer; being the difference between the 10% deposit received from, and forfeited in your favour, the buyer of your property and the total agreed sale price, as per your property sales contract, expected by you, the seller, on completion. Payment by us of your claim, and the transfer of ownership of your property to us, will constitute the completion of your property sale.
Conveyancing Fees, up to a maximum of £2,000, incurred by you and charged by your conveyancer during the period between the contractually-agreed (failed) completion date of your property sale and the date of payment of your claim and transfer of ownership of your property to us. (To also include any conveyancing fees incurred by you during the processing of your claim in the instance of ‘buyer delay’ - where your buyer successfully completes their purchase of your property, albeit late, within the time-frame set out by the Completion Notice).

Period of Financial Guarantee

The financial guarantee protection commences on the start date - the day contracts are exchanged with your buyer and your conveyancer receives from your buyer a deposit equivalent to 10% of the agreed sale value of your property. The financial guarantee will end automatically upon the first of:
90 days from the Start Date
The successful completion of your Property sale to your Buyer
The date we pay a claim under this Financial Guarantee.

Excess

This Financial Guarantee policy is excess free.

Exclusions

It is important that you understand that the Financial Guarantor bears no liability for the following exclusions that apply to the whole of this policy and that this policy does not cover:
1. If you, the vendor, decide not to sell the property prior to completion, and withdraw the Property from sale for reasons beyond your buyer’s control, or
2. Failure by your buyer to complete directly or indirectly due to a material depreciation in the condition or value of the property due to your failure to fulfil your obligations, or
3. Failure by your buyer to complete due to your failure to satisfy any outstanding conditions applied by the buyer or the buyer’s conveyancer/solicitor, or
4. Deliberate behaviour by You that results in the Buyer withdrawing from the Property sale. Such behaviour will not include normal communication involved in a sale. An example of behaviour causing the Buyer to withdraw is your seeking an increase in the agreed sale price or a change to the terms of the Agreement of Sale after you have exchanged contracts with your buyer, or
5. You, the vendor, not being legally entitled to sell the property, or
6. Physical loss or damage to your property, or
7. Loss, damage or liability arising out of a deliberate act by you or by anyone acting on your behalf, or
8. Failure to complete directly or indirectly due to:
a. Biological or chemical contamination. This includes poisoning, or preventing or limiting the use of an object, due to the effects of any biological or chemical agent; or
b. Any failure in the supply of gas, water, electricity or phone service to your property; and caused by or resulting from an act of terrorism.
9. Failure to complete directly or indirectly due to loss or distortion of information resulting from computer error or malfunction or computer virus.
10. Failure to complete directly or indirectly due to nuclear reaction, nuclear radiation or radioactive contamination.
11. Failure to complete directly or indirectly due to war, invasion, act of foreign enemies, hostilities (whether war be declared or not), civil war, rebellion, revolution, insurrection, military or usurped power.
12. Failure to complete directly or indirectly due to or resulting from your property being confiscated, taken, damaged or destroyed by or under the order of any government or public or local authority.
13. Any claim where you would be entitled to be paid under any other policy if this policy did not exist. However, this exclusion does not apply to any amount above that which would be covered under the other policy.

Making a Claim

You should immediately advise us if your buyer fails, or looks likely to fail, to complete contracts for the purchase of your property on the agreed completion date. This should be before or on the intended (failed) day of completion of your property sale. Please contact us via the ClozeSure website before or on your failed completion date. We will then start the claims process.

Alternatively, please send an email to xxxxx@xxxx.com or write to XXXX, XXXX, XXXXX.

You must give us all the co-operation we need to investigate your claim. You will need to provide us with information, including the following, to ensure we are able to meet our claims promise:
A copy of the exchanged contracts, including your Agreement of Sale
Proof of 10% deposit paid by your buyer and being held by your solicitor/conveyancer.
Your conveyancer/solicitor’s client bank account details
Name and address and contact details of:
your solicitor/conveyancer,
your estate agent,
the buyer’s solicitor/conveyancer.

On the defaulting buyer’s failure to complete on the agreed day of completion, your conveyancer will immediately issue your buyer, via the buyer’s own conveyancer, with a Completion Notice, with which the buyer must comply within, usually, 10 working days of the (missed) completion date. If the buyer fails to do so, your property contract for sale will be deemed to have failed and your defaulting buyer’s deposit should be forfeited in your favour and your claim payment will fall due.

Your claim will then be paid immediately by us to your conveyancer in exchange for the transfer, via your conveyancer and estate agent, from you to us of:
vacant possession of your property,
your property keys,
full access to your property, and
full ownership of and title to your property

From this point forth we will become the registered owners of the property.

On receipt of our settlement payment to your conveyancer they will then deduct their own fees (incurred up to the failed completion day) your estate agent’s fees and any other fees or costs due – including any outstanding mortgage charge/s – before passing on the balance of monies due to you.

Any conveyancing fees incurred by you during the period between the failed completion date and payment of our claim, will be invoiced to and paid by ourselves, up to a maximum of £2,000).

Our Claims Promise
> We pride ourselves on offering a service that is fast, efficient and helpful. Our promise to you is pay your claim immediately after, and on the same day as, your defaulting buyer fails to comply with the Completion Notice and, thus, loses the right to buy your property and forfeits their 10% deposit in your favour.We can only do this with the full cooperation of your conveyancer and you.

We appreciate that you may be simultaneously purchasing another property with the proceeds of your property sale and that you may, in turn, have received a Completion Notice from the seller of that property. We will work closely with your conveyancer, the seller’s conveyancer and any other connected parties to ensure that your onward property purchase completes successfully and the integrity of any chain of transactions remains intact.
