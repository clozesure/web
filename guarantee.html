The ClozeSure Completion Guarantee

The ClozeSure Completion Guarantee ("The Guarantee") is designed to protect the sellers of domestic property in England, Wales and Northern Ireland if their buyer withdraws between Exchange of contracts and Completion of sale.

The property purchase process - England & Wales

Why do you need the Guarantee?

If your buyer withdraws (through no fault of your own) the consequences to you can be catastrophic.

You will keep the deposit from your buyer, but;

your property will remain unsold, and:

You may lose your deposit if you have exchanged contracts on a new property but are unable to go ahead with the purchase, and face potential legal action and costs from the seller.

You will still have to pay all fees and costs due to your conveyancer.

You will still have to pay all sales fees due to your estate agent (subject to the terms of their contract).

If you had other plans for the proceeds of your sale they will be at risk.

The ClozeSure Guarantee protects you against this by stepping into the shoes of your buyer and immediately purchasing your property.

How it works

The ClozeSure Guarantee ensures that you are put back into the same financial position as you would have been had your property sale completed successfully.

It is available at the time of exchange of contracts of sale and receipt of a 10% deposit from your buyer by your conveyancer/solicitor.

It covers you from the date of purchase until the successful Completion by your buyer or 10 working days after your expected Completion Date.

It will, in the event of the failure of your buyer, pay the 90% balance of the Agreed Sale Price of your property (the remaining 10% being the deposit forfeited by your buyer in your favour).

It will reimburse you for any additional legal and conveyancing costs incurred by you, up to a maximum of £2,000, because of your buyer’s failure, or delay, to complete.

If your buyer fails to complete on the agreed Completion Date, the legal framework enables you to either 1) grant your buyer an extension or 2) serve your buyer with a valid Notice to Complete, with which they have to comply within 10 working days.

1) If you choose to grant your buyer an extension, you may request an extension to the period of your Guarantee.

If your buyer successfully completes before the end of the 10 day Notice to Complete period, the Guarantee will pay for any additional conveyancing fees you incur as a result of your buyers delay.

2) If your buyer is unable to complete by the end of the 10th day Notice period, they forfeit their 10% deposit in your favour, as per the terms of your property sale contract. The Guarantee then steps in to immediately purchase your property, pay you the remaining 90% sum and cover your additional conveyancing fees incurred due to your buyer's failure.

Peace of Mind. Guaranteed.

The Guarantee is designed to offer complete peace of mind to property sellers by making your sale, and its timing, certain from the moment of exchange of contracts and deposit.

You can also be re-assured that the Guarantee will pay out promptly, giving you the confidence to enter into any simultaneous property purchase or other time-dependent financial commitment.

Our Claims Promise

We pride ourselves on offering a service that is fast, efficient and helpful. Our promise to you is pay your claim immediately after, and on the same day as, your defaulting buyer fails to comply with the Completion Notice and, thus, loses the right to buy your property and forfeits their 10% deposit in your favour. We can only do this with the full cooperation of your conveyancer and you.

We appreciate that you may be simultaneously purchasing another property with the proceeds of your property sale and that you may, in turn, have received a Completion Notice from the seller of that property. We will work closely with your conveyancer, the seller’s conveyancer and any other connected parties to ensure that your onward property purchase completes successfully and the integrity of any chain of transactions remains intact.

Current Restrictions

Currently the Guarantee is only available to property sellers where a minimum deposit of 10% of the agreed sale price of their property has been paid.

It is not available to sellers if their buyers are not private individuals (i.e. the buyer is a corporate entity or trust of some kind) or where any of the sellers has a previous or current relationship with the buyer, whether family, social or business..

It is not currently available for commercial properties or for exchange-completion periods exceeding 3 months.

These restrictions may change in future product developments so please check back regularly if this impacts you.

Government Limitations

In the event that the Government introduces limitations placed on property purchases that cause delay to your completion date (as in the recent case of Covid19), the Guarantee period shall be frozen until the limitations are relinquished. At this point, the Guarantee period will be extended by the days remaining from the original period.
